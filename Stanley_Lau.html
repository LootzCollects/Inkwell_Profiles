STANLEY "ARTGERM" LAU

Digital Art & Design Visionary

Key Facts

  • Born: Hong Kong
  • Field: Illustrator, Designer, Concept Artist, Cover Artist
  • Debut: Early 2000s (Gaming/Illustration)
  • Notable Works:
    • Co-founder of Imaginary Friends Studios (IFS).
    • Iconic variant covers for DC Comics (e.g., Batgirl, Supergirl) and Marvel.
    • Artwork for major gaming brands including Capcom (Street Fighter) and Sony.

Biography & Influence

Stanley Lau, widely known by his pseudonym Artgerm, is a Hong Kong-born, Singapore-based illustrator whose work fuses Eastern artistic aesthetics with Western graphic design and realism. Formally trained in graphic design and advertising, Lau began his professional career in the early 2000s and quickly gained acclaim for his vibrant, highly rendered digital illustrations.

In 2005, Lau co-founded Imaginary Friends Studios (IFS), which rapidly became a world-renowned digital art studio, providing creative content for top-tier clients like Capcom, Marvel, and DC Comics. His cover artwork, characterized by photo-realistic rendering and strong emotionality, became exceptionally popular in the comic industry, particularly his distinctive variant covers for DC's female-led titles.

Lau is credited with influencing a new generation of artists, pushing a style that emphasizes visual fluidity, aesthetic perfection, and strong character portraiture. Despite being a highly sought-after commercial artist, Lau remains active in the educational sphere and maintains a significant social media presence where his art continues to inspire a global following.
