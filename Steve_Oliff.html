STEVE OLIFF

Pioneer of Digital Coloring

Key Facts

  • Born: February 20, 1954 (U.S.)
  • Field: Colorist, Writer, Separator, Publisher
  • Debut: 1978 (Marvel Comics)
  • Notable Works:
    • Colorist for Akira (English translation), pioneering computer coloring.
    • Founder of Olyoptics, the first computer color separation company.
    • Multi-time Eisner and Harvey Award winner for Best Colorist.

Biography & Influence

Steve Oliff is one of the most important figures in the history of comic book coloring. Starting his career in 1978, he initially gained recognition by coloring Bill Sienkiewicz's early Moon Knight stories. His work soon covered hundreds of titles, including Spawn, Batman, and The Maxx.

Oliff revolutionized the coloring process through his company, Olyoptics, which was the first to successfully integrate computer technology for color separation. This groundbreaking work was fully realized when he was personally selected by creator Katsuhiro Otomo to color the English translation of the landmark manga Akira. Oliff convinced **Marvel** (through its Epic Comics line) to use digital coloring on the title, a move that quickly made computer coloring the industry standard in the years that followed.

A multiple Eisner and Harvey Award winner, Oliff is renowned for mastering numerous mediums—from airbrush and gouache in his custom color guides to early digital techniques—influencing the art of comics throughout the 1990s and beyond. He is also the creator of the comic book series Armature, which appeared in **Image Comics** and as a local newspaper strip.
