Biography & Influence

Roy Thomas is a legendary writer and editor who succeeded Stan Lee as Editor-in-Chief of Marvel Comics in 1972. A lifelong comic fan and history major, Thomas was the first true "fanboy" to break into the industry, beginning his professional career at Marvel in 1965. His early work involved scripting titles from Stan Lee's plots, quickly establishing himself as a key voice of the Silver Age.

Thomas's tenure redefined numerous Marvel concepts, often infusing them with literary and mythological sophistication. He co-created an astonishing roster of characters, including the second Vision, Ultron, the original Ghost Rider (Johnny Blaze), and the iconic Canadian hero, Wolverine. He is also famous for introducing the pulp hero Conan the Barbarian to mainstream comics and for writing the pivotal Kree-Skrull War storyline in The Avengers with artist Neal Adams.

Following his time as Editor-in-Chief, Thomas famously moved to DC Comics in the 1980s, where he created the All-Star Squadron and revitalized the Golden Age DC heroes, bringing his deep historical knowledge to the Justice Society of America mythology. Throughout his career, Thomas has been highly regarded for his skill in literary adaptation, successfully converting works like The Ring of the Nibelung, the Conan films, and the original Star Wars movie into compelling comic book narratives. He continues to edit his award-winning comics-history magazine, Alter Ego.
