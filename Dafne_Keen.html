Biography & Influence

Dafne Keen is a Spanish-British actress who has become an iconic figure for modern comic book fans due to her powerful, breakthrough performance as 'Laura Kinney, the mutant clone known as X-23', in the 2017 neo-Western superhero film, 'Logan'. At just 12 years old, Keen received widespread critical acclaim for her largely non-verbal portrayal, successfully capturing the character's feral intensity, pain, and profound innocence, thereby setting a new benchmark for youth performances in the superhero genre.

Her indelible connection to the character and the comic book mythology has only solidified with her return to the role in the Marvel Cinematic Universe film, 'Deadpool & Wolverine' (2024). This reprise cemented X-23's place in the modern cinematic universe and excited fans who recognize Keen as the definitive live-action version of the character. Her performance in the 'Deadpool & Wolverine' film, where X-23 is confirmed to be the same Laura from 'Logan', has led to her signature being highly sought after on related comic book issues, particularly variants of 'X-23: Deadly Regenesis #1', demonstrating her direct, tangible influence on the collector market.

Keen continues to express a strong public desire to further explore the character of X-23 within the MCU, positioning herself as a dedicated and essential part of the X-Men's cinematic future. Beyond the comic world, she is also known for her lead role as Lyra Belacqua in the critically acclaimed HBO/BBC series 'His Dark Materials'.
