NEI RUFFINO

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: Northern California, U.S.
  • Field: Colorist, Cover Artist, Illustrator
  • Debut: Mid-2000s (Colorist work on independent titles)
  • Notable Works:
    • Colorist for major DC Comics events including Blackest Night and Final Crisis
    • Cover artist for Supergirl, Green Lantern, and Batman (DC)
    • Creator of the original comic series The Secret Life of Crows

Biography & Influence

Nei Ruffino began her artistic journey as a tattoo artist but quickly transitioned to the comic book industry, where she built a strong reputation as a highly sought-after **colorist** and **cover artist**. Her work is characterized by vibrant palettes, strong contrast, and a distinctive style that brings depth to both line art and full-color illustration.

She is widely recognized for her prolific coloring work across both Marvel and DC Comics, contributing colors to major titles such as Spider-Man, Green Lantern, and Supergirl. Ruffino has been entrusted with coloring the pencils of major industry artists like J. Scott Campbell, Frank Cho, and Eric Basaldua, showcasing her versatility in adapting to different styles.

Beyond her coloring career, Ruffino is an established illustrator and creator, most notably as the artist and creator of the fantasy comic The Secret Life of Crows. She has also contributed design and illustration work to non-comic properties like Hasbro's My Little Pony and Littlest Pet Shop. Ruffino's dedication to her craft is evident in her live-streamed art sessions, where she engages directly with her fan community.
