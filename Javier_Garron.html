JAVIER GARRÓN

Comic Book Professional

  • Field: Penciller, Designer, Concept Artist
  • Debut: 2014 (DC Comics)
  • Notable Works:
    • Miles Morales: Spider-Man

    • Ant-Man & The Wasp
    • Marvel's Spider-Man: Miles Morales (Game)

Biography & Influence

Javier Garrón is a Spanish artist who studied architecture before dedicating himself to comic book illustration, quickly rising to prominence for his dynamic, expressive, and detailed superhero art. After early work at DC Comics on titles like *Batgirl* and *Batman Eternal*, he became an exclusive artist for Marvel, where he was designated a "Young Gun" (2018-2019) for his potential. Garrón is celebrated for his unique ability to balance explosive action with highly relatable, human emotion in large-scale narratives.

He is best known for relaunching the *Miles Morales: Spider-Man* series with Saladin Ahmed, a critical run that further defined the character's voice for a new generation. Following this, he became the rare consistent main artist for three years on the flagship *Avengers* title with writer Jason Aaron, solidifying his reputation as a powerhouse sequential storyteller. Garrón's influence extends into other media, having contributed character designs to the **Marvel's Spider-Man: Miles Morales** video game and creating illustrations for *Fortnite*.

Miles Morales & The Future of Spider-Man

During this NYCC 2018 interview, Javier Garrón discusses the enormous commitment and unique artistic challenges of drawing *Miles Morales: Spider-Man*. He explains that while Peter Parker represents the parallel development of superhero and fan over decades, Miles embodies **hope and the future**, offering a fresh, "innocent" perspective that appeals to new audiences. Garrón emphasizes that drawing Miles, who is still a teenager, requires a specific, leaner body language and expression. He praises writer Saladin Ahmed for crafting thoughtful scripts that are open to collaboration and pay careful attention to both intimate character emotions and the timing of large action set-pieces, confirming that drawing the series has been a dream come true.
