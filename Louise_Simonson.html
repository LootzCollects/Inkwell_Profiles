LOUISE SIMONSON

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: September 26, 1946 (Mary Louise Alexander)
  • Field: Writer, Editor (Nickname: "Weezie")
  • Debut: 1974 (Warren Publishing, later Marvel Editor)
  • Notable Works:
    • Co-Creator of Cable, Apocalypse, Steel, and Doomsday
    • Architect of the "Mutant Massacre" storyline
    • Creator of Power Pack

Biography & Influence

Louise Simonson is a highly acclaimed writer and editor, pivotal to shaping both the Marvel and DC Universes over several decades. Beginning her career as an editor for Warren Publishing and then Marvel (where she oversaw titles like Uncanny X-Men and Star Wars), she transitioned to writing full-time in the mid-1980s.

She launched the innovative title Power Pack, featuring four pre-teen superheroes, which explored surprisingly mature themes. On the X-Men line, she gained fame writing X-Factor and New Mutants, co-creating enduring characters such as Cable and the villain Apocalypse (in X-Factor #6). Her suggestion to expand the "Mutant Massacre" storyline across multiple titles established the modern, interconnected comic book crossover event.

In the 1990s, Simonson moved to DC Comics to co-write Superman: The Man of Steel. Here, she played a key role in the iconic "Death of Superman" storyline, co-creating the formidable villain Doomsday and introducing the hero Steel. Simonson is admired for her focus on character emotion, inner conflict, and her belief that characters must face real consequences for a story to resonate with the reader.

Interview & Commentary

In this interview, Louise Simonson (Weezie) discusses her career path, including her early days modeling for Bernie Wrightson's first Swamp Thing cover and how she became the co-creator of Apocalypse and Doomsday. She delves into the theme of conflict, explaining that the most interesting superhero stories explore why characters need one thing but want another. She also touches on the collaborative effort behind "The Death of Superman" and the satisfaction of seeing her created character, Steel, become a lasting part of the DC mythos.
