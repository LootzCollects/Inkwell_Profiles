Biography & Influence

Joe Kelly (Joseph Kelly) is an acclaimed American comic book writer, editor, and co-creator of the Man of Action creative studio. He is noted for his complex character work and ability to inject both high-concept superhero action and deeply personal drama into his stories. He began his career in 1995 as a graduate of the Marvel/NYU "Stan-Hattan Project" writing workshop. His early work for Marvel included a transformative run on Deadpool, where he defined the character's signature blend of dark humor, fourth-wall breaking, and emotional depth, which is still considered the industry standard and heavily influences the character's depiction in other media.

Kelly is equally renowned for his impactful work at DC Comics, particularly his run on Action Comics, which included the highly praised Action Comics #775 ("What's So Funny About Truth, Justice & the American Way?"). He later returned to Marvel for the Brand New Day era of Amazing Spider-Man, penning memorable arcs like "Rage of the Rhino" and "Grim Hunt." Beyond mainstream comics, Kelly has earned critical acclaim for original graphic novels such as I Kill Giants and Four Eyes.

As a co-founder of Man of Action Studios, he and his partners co-created the global hit animated series Ben 10 and Generator Rex for Cartoon Network, demonstrating his versatile success across comics, film, and television. His influence continues today as the current writer of the mainline Amazing Spider-Man title.
