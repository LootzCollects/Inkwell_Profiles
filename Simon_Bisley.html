Biography & Influence

Simon Bisley is a British comic book artist renowned for his unique, aggressive, and highly kinetic painted style, which blended influences from Frank Frazetta, graffiti art, and heavy metal album covers. Largely self-taught, he began his professional career with the iconic British magazine 2000 AD in 1987, starting on the ABC Warriors strip.

Bisley's rise to international fame was secured by his fully painted work on Sláine: The Horned God and his defining artistic reinvention of DC Comics' Lobo. Initially a minor character, Bisley transformed Lobo into the monstrous, muscular, leather-clad biker seen in the successful early 1990s miniseries like Lobo: The Last Czarnian and Lobo's Back, making him an anarchic counter-cultural icon of the era. Reflecting on this period, Bisley stated he felt a deep personal connection, saying, "I didn't just create Lobo, I was Lobo!" at the time, channeling his own wild lifestyle into the character's excess and violence.

His dynamic, mixed-media work led him to illustrate the first major crossover graphic novel, Batman/Judge Dredd: Judgement on Gotham, and he remains a perennial influence, with his style inspiring characters in media like the BBC's Doctor Who and the main character in the sitcom Spaced.
