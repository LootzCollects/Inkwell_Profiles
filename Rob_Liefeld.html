ROB LIEFELD

Comic Book Professional

  • Field: Artist, Writer, Publisher, Co-Founder of Image Comics

  • Notable Works:

Biography & Influence

Rob Liefeld is one of the most prominent and polarizing figures in modern comic book history, known for his dynamic, exaggerated art style that defined the aesthetic of the 1990s. Born in California, Liefeld's passion for comics led him to become a self-taught artist, often citing the influence of creators like George PÃ©rez and Frank Miller.

He rose to prominence quickly at Marvel Comics, taking over The New Mutants in 1989 and transforming the struggling title into the massively successful X-Force. During this run, he co-created two of his most enduring characters: the time-traveling mutant Cable (with Louise Simonson) and the highly popular mercenary Deadpool (with Fabian Nicieza). Liefeld's success was instrumental in the founding of Image Comics in 1992, where he and six other superstar artists broke away from Marvel to establish a creator-owned revolution. His launch title for his own imprint, Extreme Studios, was Youngblood #1, which shattered industry sales records.

While his kinetic style, characterized by oversized musculature and an abundance of pouches and accessories, has drawn criticism over the years, his commercial impact is undeniable. He returned to Marvel periodically, notably with the "Heroes Reborn" project and the Major X series, and his creations continue to thrive in film and media. Liefeld remains a strong advocate for creator rights and actively works on his creator-owned properties, cementing his legacy as a powerful force in both the art and business of comics.
