Biography & Influence

Ben Templesmith is an Australian comic book artist and writer renowned for his unique, often macabre, and instantly recognizable digital painting style. His work typically features heavy, dark shadows, moody watercolor textures, and visceral imagery, making him a perfect fit for the horror and crime genres. He eschews traditional comic book line-art in favor of a raw, expressionistic approach.

Templesmith's breakthrough came with the critically acclaimed horror series **30 Days of Night** (co-created with writer Steve Niles), which was later adapted into a major motion picture. He has since become a staple in creator-owned comics, including the award-winning series **Fell** (with Warren Ellis) and his own creation, **Wormwood: Gentleman Corpse**. His signature style has been highly influential in independent comics, pushing the boundaries of what sequential art can look like.
