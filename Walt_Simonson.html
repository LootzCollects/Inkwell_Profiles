WALT SIMONSON

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: September 2, 1946 (Tennessee, U.S.)
  • Field: Writer, Artist, Penciller, Inker
  • Debut: 1973 (DC Comics, Weird War Tales)
  • Notable Works:
    • Defining writer/artist on Marvel’s Thor (1983-1987), co-creating Beta Ray Bill.
    • Co-creator of the Time Variance Authority (TVA) and the original Manhunter (Paul Kirk revival).
    • Sequential adaptation artist for Alien, Star Wars, and Robocop vs. Terminator.

Biography & Influence

Walter Simonson is one of the most influential comic book creators of the late Bronze and early Modern Ages, known for his dynamic, blocky art style and epic storytelling. Initially studying geology with the intent to become a paleontologist, Simonson’s passion for Jack Kirby’s work on Thor led him to the Rhode Island School of Design, graduating in 1972.

His career began at DC Comics, where he worked with writer Archie Goodwin to revive the Golden Age character Manhunter in Detective Comics, an acclaimed series that cemented his reputation as an excellent artist. Simonson cemented his legendary status at Marvel Comics with his celebrated run as both writer and artist on The Mighty Thor (1983-1987). This run is considered a comic book benchmark, introducing enduring characters like the alien hero Beta Ray Bill, exploring high-fantasy themes like Ragnarok, and even temporarily turning Thor into the "Frog of Thunder."

Simonson is known for ingeniously integrating the shared universe, introducing the Time Variance Authority (TVA) in Thor, which would later gain global recognition through media adaptations. His ability to blend superhero action with deep mythological and science fiction concepts is unrivaled. He is married to writer Louise Simonson, and the two have collaborated on titles such as X-Factor, and memorably made a cameo together in the 2011 Thor feature film.
