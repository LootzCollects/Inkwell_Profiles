Comic Book Professional

Biography & Influence

Scott Koblish is an accomplished American comic book artist, inker, and writer who has worked on over 600 comics for major publishers. Koblish trained at The Joe Kubert School and the School of Visual Arts before beginning his career as part of Marvel's renowned **Romita Raiders** apprenticeship program under John Romita Sr. in 1993.

Initially establishing himself as a reliable inker on titles like G.I. Joe, The Punisher, and DC's OMAC, Koblish gained his greatest fame for his six-year run as the primary artist on **Marvel's** flagship Deadpool series. His ability to render vast, intricate scenes culminated in securing the Guinness World Record for the most characters ever depicted on a single comic book cover with Deadpool #27. This era also saw him co-create the character Deadpool 2099.

Known for his chameleon-like skill, Koblish can seamlessly adapt his style to suit different genres and eras, often paying homage to classic comic art.
