PHILLIP KENNEDY JOHNSON

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: 1979 (Iowa, U.S.)
  • Field: Writer (Comics, Graphic Novels), Master Sergeant (U.S. Army), Musician
  • Debut: 2015 (BOOM! Studios' Last Sons of America)
  • Notable Works:
    • Acclaimed run on Action Comics and Superman ("Warworld Saga").
    • Writer for Marvel's Alien and The Incredible Hulk.
    • Co-creator of DC Black Label's The Last God.

Biography & Influence

Phillip Kennedy Johnson is an Eisner-nominated writer celebrated for his high-stakes, epic storytelling that often blends science fiction and horror with core superhero concepts. Johnson's unique background heavily informs his work: before becoming a full-time comics writer, he held a dual career as a Master Sergeant in the **United States Army Field Band**, serving as a trumpet player and composer.

His entry into comics was spurred by his desire to help his brother break into the industry. Johnson quickly found success with independent titles like **BOOM! Studios'** Last Sons of America (which is in development at Netflix) and Warlords of Appalachia. This led to work with major publishers, including launching a celebrated run on **Marvel's** Alien and, most notably, being entrusted with the Superman line at **DC Comics**.

Johnson's acclaimed work on Action Comics and Superman, particularly the epic "Warworld Saga," was a massive undertaking that redefined the Man of Steel's mission on a cosmic scale. His writing is characterized by exploring the philosophical and human elements of superheroism, often creating powerful villains who operate on a warped moral code. He maintains a busy schedule across the industry, currently writing **Marvel's** The Incredible Hulk and **DC's** Batman and Robin.
