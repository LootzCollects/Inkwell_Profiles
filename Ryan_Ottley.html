RYAN OTTLEY

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: 1975 (U.S.)
  • Field: Artist, Writer, Co-creator (Invincible, Grizzlyshark)
  • Debut: Early 2000s (Independent comics/webstrips)
  • Notable Works:
    • Primary artist for 127 issues of Invincible.
    • Long runs on Marvel's The Amazing Spider-Man and Hulk.
    • Co-creator of characters like Battle Beast and Conquest.

Biography & Influence

Ryan Ottley is a highly successful American comic book artist renowned for his dynamic, visceral, and emotionally expressive sequential art. He is best known for his definitive 14-year run alongside writer Robert Kirkman on the Image Comics series, Invincible. Ottley's ability to blend cartoonish expressiveness with shockingly detailed violence defined the tone of the long-running superhero epic, with his artwork gracing 127 out of the series' 144 issues.

Ottley's career breakthrough came unexpectedly after he was fired from a warehouse job. He began actively building exposure for his web-strip, Ted Noodleman: Bicycle Delivery Boy, which caught the attention of Robert Kirkman. This connection led him to become the primary artist for Invincible, where he co-created key characters like Battle Beast, Angstrom Levy, and Conquest, cementing his place as a creative monolith of the modern comic era.

Following the conclusion of Invincible in 2018, Ottley moved to Marvel Comics, where he quickly became a fan-favorite artist on flagship titles, illustrating successful runs on both The Amazing Spider-Man (co-creating the villain Kindred) and Hulk. In addition to his collaborative work, Ottley is the writer and artist of the creator-owned horror-comedy series, Grizzlyshark. He currently serves as a creative consultant for the critically acclaimed animated adaptation of Invincible on Amazon Prime Video, further emphasizing his creative ownership of the property.
