MARC SILVESTRI

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: March 29, 1958 (Palm Beach, Florida, U.S.)
  • Field: Penciller, Writer, Publisher, Co-Founder of Image Comics
  • Debut: 1981 (DC's House of Mystery)
  • Notable Works:
    • Co-Creator of Image Comics and CEO of Top Cow Productions
    • Co-Creator of Witchblade, The Darkness, and Cyberforce
    • Iconic runs on Uncanny X-Men and Wolverine

Biography & Influence

Marc Silvestri is one of the most recognizable and influential comic book artists of the late 20th and early 21st centuries. He is a co-founder of Image Comics and currently serves as the **CEO of Top Cow Productions**, his own publishing imprint within Image. Silvestri began his career at DC Comics before moving to Marvel, where he gained massive popularity for his dynamic and highly detailed work on the flagship X-titles.

His definitive run on Uncanny X-Men in the late 1980s solidified his place as a superstar, followed by a two-year stint penciling the solo Wolverine title alongside writer Larry Hama. In 1992, at the peak of his career, Silvestri joined six other high-profile artists to form Image Comics, a revolutionary move that prioritized creator-owned properties.

Under his Top Cow imprint, Silvestri created and co-created numerous successful franchises that defined the era, including Cyberforce, Witchblade, and The Darkness. He later branched into producing film and television adaptations of his properties, such as the Witchblade TV series and the highly successful The Darkness video games. Silvestri continues to work extensively, recently writing, penciling, and inking the acclaimed DC miniseries, Batman & The Joker: The Deadly Duo.
