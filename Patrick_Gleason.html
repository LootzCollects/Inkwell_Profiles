PATRICK GLEASON

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: United States
  • Field: Penciller, Writer, Cover Artist
  • Debut: 1999 (Marvel Comics' X-Men Unlimited #22)
  • Notable Works:
    • Co-creator of the modern Super Sons (DC) and Chasm (Marvel).
    • Artist/Writer on Superman: Rebirth and Robin: Son of Batman (DC).
    • Highly acclaimed runs on Green Lantern Corps and Batman and Robin (DC).

Biography & Influence

Patrick "Pat" Gleason is a New York Times best-selling American comic book artist and writer known for his emotionally rich, kinetic art style and his successful runs on some of the biggest titles at DC and Marvel Comics. His work often blends epic superhero action with heartfelt character dynamics, especially father-son relationships.

Gleason built his early career on titles like DC's Green Lantern Corps and the major event Blackest Night, but his popularity surged during the New 52 era, particularly with writer Peter J. Tomasi on Batman and Robin. This run cemented his reputation for dynamic figure work and emotional storytelling, leading to his later role as writer and artist on Robin: Son of Batman.

In 2016, Gleason and Tomasi were paired again as the writer/artist team for the critically acclaimed Superman: Rebirth series. This run, which focused on Clark Kent becoming a father and the creation of the character Jon Kent (the Super Son), is often hailed as a modern classic. After years as a DC exclusive artist, Gleason moved to Marvel, bringing his unique energy to titles like The Amazing Spider-Man, for which he created the popular villain Chasm. Gleason's contributions are consistently praised for both their storytelling and character design.
