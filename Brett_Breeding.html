Biography & Influence

Brett Breeding is one of the most prolific inkers in modern comic history, best known for his long and defining run on the Superman titles in the 1980s and 1990s. He started his career at Marvel Comics as an assistant to Bob Layton, where he quickly moved on to inking for titles like *Iron Man*, *The Avengers*, and *The Amazing Spider-Man*. His early partnerships, particularly with penciler Ron Frenz, established his reputation for clean, powerful line work.

Breeding's career took a significant turn when he moved to DC Comics, where he became the primary inker for penciler Dan Jurgens on the flagship *Superman* titles. This partnership was crucial in visually defining Superman for the Post-Crisis generation, lending a clean, iconic feel to the character's adventures. Their collaboration peaked with the industry-shaking **"Death of Superman"** storyline, where Breeding's inking amplified the drama and impact of Jurgens' pencils, making him an integral part of one of the best-selling comics events of all time.
