Biography & Influence

Marguerite Bennett is a highly successful and prolific American comic book writer known for her distinct voice, particularly in championing diverse, female-led narratives. She began her career after studying under celebrated writer Scott Snyder at Sarah Lawrence College, leading to her professional debut on Batman Annual #2 in 2013.

Bennett quickly rose to prominence writing for both Marvel and DC, but she is most celebrated for her work on **DC Comics' Bombshells**, an alternate universe title featuring an all-female cast of heroes reimagined in a World War II setting. This series, along with others like Batwoman and Marvel's all-female A-Force (co-written with G. Willow Wilson), earned her multiple GLAAD Media Award nominations for her nuanced portrayal of female relationships and LGBTQ+ characters.

In the creator-owned space, she achieved commercial success with Animosity (AfterShock Comics), a critically acclaimed series exploring a world where animals suddenly gain human consciousness. Her work often explores darker, complex themes, exemplified by her Victorian-era erotic horror series, InSeXts, reflecting her commitment to challenging the traditional landscape of mainstream comics.

Interview & Commentary

In this segment, host Laura from *Married with Comics* discusses the themes in Bennett’s independent comic, The Lot, and how it effectively articulates the challenges of being a woman in a male-dominated professional world. The commentary praises Bennett for using the horror genre not just for spectacle, but as an insightful layer to explore the main character’s struggle against both men and other women who uphold frustrating status quo assumptions, proving that insightful, feminist narratives can thrive outside of being niche or tokenized.
