EDDIE NUNEZ

Comic Book Professional

  • Notable Works:
    • DC Universe Online (Concept Design)

Biography & Influence

Eddie Nunez is an American artist recognized for his versatile work as a penciler, inker, and character designer across comics, video games, and animation. Born and raised in El Paso, Texas, Nunez was heavily influenced by classic Japanese anime, Western comic books, and video games like *Street Fighter*. This blend is evident in his signature style, which utilizes large, chunky shapes and dynamic posing to give his characters a robust and powerful aesthetic.

Nunez launched his professional career in 2008 when he was hired to work for WildStorm/DC Comics, focusing primarily on concept design and artwork for **DC Universe Online**. He has lent his artistic touch to numerous mainstream comic titles, including Flashpoint: Lois Lane and the Resistance, Deadpool, Batman/Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles, and currently contributes to series like Masters of the Universe: Masterverse for Dark Horse. His background in character design also led him to Austin, Texas, to work on the Netflix animated series Seis Manos.

A popular face on the convention circuit, Nunez's influence comes from his ability to adapt classic properties into modern, visually strong designs, making his work highly sought after by fans and collaborators alike. He regularly shares his process and art through conventions and sketchbooks, continually stressing the importance of "keep drawing" to develop an artist's own library of shapes and forms.

Interview & Commentary

In this interview from NYCC 2018, Eddie Nunez confirms his work on the new animated series *Seis Manos*, noting that while he couldn't share many details, the show takes place in Mexico and had a projected release date of August 2019. He discusses his artistic influences, citing *Street Fighter* and old Disney cartoons for inspiring his love of "big chunky shapes" in character design. Nunez mentions his professional start at WildStorm/DC in 2008 and his heavy presence on the convention circuit, including San Diego Comic-Con, to connect directly with fans and share his art.
