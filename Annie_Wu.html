Biography & Influence

Annie Wu is an acclaimed artist and illustrator known for her distinct, fashion-forward, and expressive style. Her art often features a raw, stylish energy characterized by strong lines, dynamic posing, and a keen eye for modern design. Wuâ€™s clean yet edgy aesthetic quickly caught the attention of major publishers, establishing her as a unique voice among modern comic book artists.

She rose to prominence through her work on Matt Fraction and David Aja's definitive run on **Hawkeye** for Marvel Comics, where she contributed to the highly celebrated "Pizza Dog" issue and other key chapters. Her sequential art is particularly praised for its ability to convey complex character emotions and narratives with minimal detail, relying instead on composition and visual flair. Following *Hawkeye*, she became the primary artist for the launch of DC Comics' **Black Canary** series, which was celebrated for its rock-and-roll sensibility.
