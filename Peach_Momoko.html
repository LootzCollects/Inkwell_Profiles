PEACH MOMOKO

Comic Book Professional

    • Ongoing writer and artist for Ultimate X-Men (Marvel).

Biography & Influence

Peach Momoko is a Japanese comic book artist and writer known for her distinctive, dream-like watercolor style, often blending themes of haunting beauty, femininity, and Japanese folklore. Her unique approach rapidly established her as one of the most popular cover artists in the industry.

After studying video game design and working on various independent art projects, Momoko was introduced to the American comic industry by artist Adi Granov. Her career exploded when she began producing a high volume of variant covers for Marvel Comics, earning her a spot in the prestigious **Marvel Stormbreakers** program. She received widespread acclaim and multiple awards, including the **Eisner Award for Best Cover Artist** in 2021 and 2024.

Momoko cemented her creative independence by developing the "Momoko-verse," starting with the series Demon Days, which she wrote and illustrated entirely herself. These stories successfully reimagined major Marvel characters through the aesthetic and lens of Japanese *yokai* (supernatural beings) and folktales. She has continued this work as the writer and artist for the Ultimate Comics relaunch of Ultimate X-Men.
