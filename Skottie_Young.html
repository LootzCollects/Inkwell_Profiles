SKOTTIE YOUNG

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: March 3, 1978 (Fairbury, Illinois, U.S.)
  • Field: Writer, Artist, Cartoonist, Children's Book Illustrator
  • Debut: 2001 (10th Muse / Marvel Mangaverse)
  • Notable Works:
    • Creator-owned series I Hate Fairyland and Middlewest.
    • Hugely popular "Baby Variant" covers for Marvel Comics.
    • Eisner Award-winning adaptations of L. Frank Baum's Oz Books.

Biography & Influence

Skottie Young is an award-winning American cartoonist, writer, and artist whose distinct, high-energy, and often humorous style has made him one of the most commercially successful creators in the industry. After beginning his career with fill-in and backup work for Marvel and Image Comics in the early 2000s, Young gradually cultivated a unique artistic voice that broke away from traditional superhero aesthetics.

His career trajectory shifted dramatically with his Eisner Award-winning work adapting L. Frank Baum's Oz books for Marvel, establishing him as a top-tier illustrator and leading to his groundbreaking run on Rocket Raccoon. However, Young is arguably best known for his series of "Baby Variant" covers, which depict adult Marvel characters as humorous infants. These covers became a massive commercial phenomenon, earning him the Inkpot Award and cementing his status as a Marvel cash cow.

Young successfully leveraged his commercial popularity into a celebrated career as a creator-owner. Through his company, Stupid Fresh Mess, he writes and illustrates his hit series I Hate Fairyland, a dark comedy that satirizes fantasy tropes. His other notable creator-owned works include the emotional Western Ain't No Grave and the critically praised fantasy Middlewest. Young is recognized as a savvy businessman, demonstrating how creators can effectively manage their brand and sales independently to retain creative control and maximize profits.
