JOHN BEATTY

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: Letcher County, Kentucky
  • Field: Inker, Finisher, Penciler
  • Debut: 1980 (Age 19, Marvel/DC)
  • Notable Works:
    • Marvel Super Heroes: Secret Wars
    • The Punisher (1986 Mini-Series)
    • Batman (with Kelley Jones)

Biography & Influence

John Beatty is a veteran American comic book artist best known as one of the most prolific and technically brilliant **inkers** of the Bronze and Modern Ages. Breaking into the industry at age 19 in 1980, he quickly gained prominence for his clean, precise, and tight inking style, which complemented a wide array of pencilers. His career began at DC Comics, where he worked as a finisher on JLA over pencils by **George Pérez**.

Beatty is perhaps most famous for his extensive and decades-long collaboration with penciler **Mike Zeck**. Their legendary partnership defined the look of major 1980s Marvel events and series, including the 12-issue landmark crossover Marvel Super Heroes: Secret Wars (1984)—which introduced the black suit that eventually led to Venom—and the acclaimed Punisher mini-series (1986). Their run on Captain America in the early 80s remains a fan favorite.

In the 1990s, Beatty was a cornerstone artist for DC Comics’ darker titles, cementing his status with titles like Batman: Year 3 and an iconic three-year run on the monthly Batman book over the highly stylized pencils of **Kelley Jones**. His versatility and clean lines have made him a highly sought-after artist across almost every major character and publisher in the industry.
