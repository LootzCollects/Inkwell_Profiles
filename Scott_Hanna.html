SCOTT HANNA

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: 1962 (U.S.)
  • Field: Inker, Artist, Illustrator, Educator
  • Debut: 1988 (DC Comics)
  • Notable Works:
    • Eisner Award-winning Inker (Amazing Spider-Man: "Coming Home").
    • Five-year run on Detective Comics (Batman) and extensive work on Green Lantern Corps.
    • Inked over 23,000 pages of comics art across Marvel and DC.

Biography & Influence

Scott Hanna is one of the most prolific and recognized inkers in the comic book industry, known for his ability to adapt his style to virtually any penciler while maintaining a high standard of detail and clarity. Hanna graduated from Pratt Institute and built a career that has seen him ink over 23,000 pages of sequential art for Marvel and DC Comics.

Hanna's career highlights include a successful five-year run on DC's Detective Comics and a long association with the Green Lantern Corps mythos. At Marvel, he became heavily involved in the Spider-Man franchise, famously collaborating with John Romita Jr. on the acclaimed Amazing Spider-Man story arc "Coming Home," which earned him an Eisner Award for Best Serialized Story in 2002. His work on Ultimate Spider-Man proved particularly influential, with animators and designers later using his artistic interpretation as a foundation for various media adaptations, including the Spider-Verse films.

Hanna views the inking process as a crucial and collaborative art form that requires artists to know how to draw and understand line weight, texture, and light sourcing to enhance the penciler's storytelling. In addition to his comic book career, Hanna is a third-generation artist and an active educator, co-founding the Arts and Fashion Institute in Pennsylvania, where he teaches sequential art and traditional drawing techniques.
