TOM ORZECHOWSKI

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: March 1, 1953 (Michigan, U.S.)
  • Field: Letterer, Copy Editor, Designer
  • Debut: 1973 (Marvel UK, lettering retouches)
  • Notable Works:
    • Record-holder for lettering the most issues of a single series (Spawn).
    • Defining letterer for Chris Claremont’s Uncanny X-Men (6,000+ pages).
    • Localization and lettering work on early manga translations (Nausicaä, Ghost in the Shell).

Biography & Influence

Tom Orzechowski is one of the most respected and prolific letterers in the history of American comics, credited with lettering over 1,500 issues across his career. His work began in the fan community before he was hired by Marvel Comics in 1973 to retouch and re-letter American comics for the British market.

He rose to prominence as the defining letterer for Chris Claremont's legendary run on Uncanny X-Men, starting in 1979. Over a 25-year period, he lettered roughly 6,000 pages of Claremont's work across flagship titles like X-Men, The New Mutants, and Wolverine, creating the distinctive look that many fans associate with the franchise's Silver and Bronze Age period. His style, influenced by 1930s Flash Gordon strips, is characterized by precise, square-ish, easily legible, superhero-style calligraphy.

After leaving Marvel, Orzechowski became the copy editor and primary letterer for Image Comics' launch title Spawn in 1992, lettering over 350 issues of the series. This enduring contribution earned him the Guinness World Record for lettering the most issues of a single comic book series. He also played a key role in the early mainstream localization of manga, lettering titles such as Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind and Ghost in the Shell.
