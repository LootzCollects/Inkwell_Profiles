RICK LEONARDI

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: August 9, 1957 (Philadelphia, U.S.)
  • Field: Artist, Penciller
  • Debut: 1981 (Marvel's Thor #303)
  • Notable Works:
    • Co-creator of Spider-Man 2099 (with Peter David).
    • Work on The Uncanny X-Men (co-created Genosha with Chris Claremont).
    • Co-designed Spider-Man's Black Costume (with Mike Zeck).

Biography & Influence

Rick Leonardi is a veteran American comic book artist known for his fluid, dynamic line work and strong storytelling ability, traits he admired early in the work of Joe Kubert. Leonardi began his career in 1981 at Marvel Comics and quickly established himself with key work on various fill-in issues before becoming the long-time penciler for Cloak and Dagger.

Leonardi is most famous for his role in defining a futuristic corner of the Marvel Universe as the regular penciler for Spider-Man 2099 (with writer Peter David), designing the titular hero's iconic costume. He also made significant contributions to the X-titles, including long runs on The Uncanny X-Men and The New Mutants, and co-created the fictional nation of Genosha with Chris Claremont. A little-known fact is that Leonardi and Mike Zeck were integral to designing the famous black-and-white costume Spider-Man acquired during the Secret Wars miniseries.

Throughout his career, Leonardi has worked with numerous major writers and moved between Marvel and DC, lending his talents to DC books like Nightwing, Batgirl, and Superman. His continued output and clean, action-oriented style have cemented his legacy as one of the most reliable and influential pencilers of the Modern Age.
