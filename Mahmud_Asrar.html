MAHMUD ASRAR

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: November 20, 1976 (Ankara, Turkey)
  • Field: Penciller, Inker, Co-Creator (Turkey/Austria)
  • Debut: Early 2000s; Dynamo 5 (Image Comics, 2007)
  • Notable Works:
    • Supergirl and Batman vs. Robin
    • Conan the Barbarian and King Conan
    • X-Men Red and All-New X-Men

Biography & Influence

Mahmud Asrar is a prolific and acclaimed Turkish-Austrian comic book artist recognized for his dynamic and highly detailed sequential storytelling. After studying graphic design and animation, he launched an international career, quickly becoming a sought-after talent at major publishers, including Image Comics, Marvel, and DC.

His career took off with the creator-owned series Dynamo 5 (co-created with writer Jay Faerber) for Image Comics. He subsequently became a key artist during DC's "New 52" era, defining the look for Supergirl, and later contributed significantly to DC events like Batman vs. Robin.

Asrar is perhaps best known for his work at Marvel, notably for his acclaimed runs on various X-Men titles (including All-New X-Men and X-Men Red) and for his award-winning work on Conan the Barbarian and King Conan. His style is characterized by a strong sense of kinetic energy and expressive character anatomy, making him ideal for both large-scale fantasy and superhero action.
