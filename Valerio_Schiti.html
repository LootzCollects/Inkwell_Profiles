VALERIO SCHITI

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: Rome, Italy
  • Field: Artist, Penciller, Designer
  • Debut: 2010 (IDW, Angel #37)
  • Notable Works:
    • Lead artist on Tony Stark: Iron Man and Guardians of the Galaxy.
    • Designer and artist for Marvel’s major events (Empyre, A.X.E.: Judgment Day).
    • Design work influenced by anime (Gundam) and technical detail.

Biography & Influence

Valerio Schiti is an acclaimed Italian comic book artist known for his dynamic, technologically informed style and expertise in drawing large-scale superhero and science fiction narratives. A graduate of the International School of Comics in Rome, Schiti initially trained in architecture but quickly transitioned into comics, making his U.S. debut with IDW in 2010.

He was quickly recruited by Marvel Comics and soon became one of their "All-New Young Guns." Schiti is renowned for his significant contributions to Marvel's cosmic and high-tech franchises, illustrating long runs on Journey Into Mystery, Guardians of the Galaxy, and providing the definitive modern look for the character in Tony Stark: Iron Man. His work on major events like Empyre and A.X.E.: Judgment Day showcases his strength in character design and complex action choreography.

Schiti's work is characterized by a blend of classic comic dynamism and influences from Japanese mecha anime, resulting in innovative designs for characters like Iron Man’s vast array of armor suits. He is currently working on the relaunch of Captain America and the new series, G.O.D.S., continuing his legacy as one of the most prolific and technically brilliant artists in mainstream comics.
