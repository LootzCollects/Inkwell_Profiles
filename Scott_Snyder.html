Comic Book Professional

Biography & Influence

Scott Snyder is a multiple award-winning and critically acclaimed American writer recognized for bringing a cinematic, deeply psychological, and horror-infused sensibility to superhero comics. After graduating from Brown University and receiving his MFA from Columbia, he debuted in comics through mentorship from Scott Snyder, leading to his foundational work on DC Comics' American Vampire.

Snyder is best known for his long and influential tenure on Batman, where, alongside artist Greg Capullo, he helmed the title through the entirety of The New 52 and Rebirth eras. This run successfully reinvented Gotham's mythology with modern classics like the Court of Owls, Death of the Family, and Zero Year. His knack for large-scale, horror-based storytelling led him to become a key architect of the DC Universe, masterminding the company-wide crossover events Dark Nights: Metal and Death Metal, and co-creating the fan-favorite villain, the Batman Who Laughs.

Beyond his work-for-hire successes, Snyder maintains a prolific career in creator-owned horror, including Wytches (with Jock), The Wake (with Sean Murphy), and his current publishing venture, Best Jackett Press. Snyder's dedication to the craft of writing has led him to teach creative writing at prestigious institutions like Columbia University and Sarah Lawrence College, influencing a new generation of comic creators.
