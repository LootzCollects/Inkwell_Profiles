TODD MCFARLANE

Publisher and Visionary of Image Comics

Key Facts

  • Born: March 16, 1961 (Calgary, Canada)
  • Field: Artist, Writer, Publisher, Entrepreneur
  • Debut: 1984 (Marvel Comics, Scorpio Rose)
  • Notable Works:
    • Co-founder and President of Image Comics; founder of McFarlane Toys.
    • Creator of the best-selling independent comic, Spawn.
    • Influential run on The Amazing Spider-Man, co-creating Venom.

Biography & Influence

Todd McFarlane is one of the most influential and successful figures in modern comic book history, known for transforming the industry both artistically and professionally. After suffering a career-ending injury in baseball, McFarlane turned his full attention to comics, persevering through over 700 rejection letters before landing his first assignment in 1984.

He achieved superstar status at Marvel Comics in the late 1980s for his work on The Incredible Hulk and, most famously, The Amazing Spider-Man. On Spider-Man, McFarlane redesigned the web-slinger with larger eyes and dynamic, spaghetti-like webbing, visually defining the character for a new generation. He is credited as the co-creator and designer of the iconic anti-hero Venom. His first issue writing and drawing his own title, simply titled Spider-Man #1 (1990), sold a record-breaking 2.5 million copies.

In 1992, frustrated with the lack of creator rights and ownership at established publishers, McFarlane and six other star artists co-founded Image Comics. His studio, Todd McFarlane Productions, launched Spawn, an occult horror anti-hero he had created in high school. Spawn #1 sold an unprecedented 1.7 million copies, becoming the best-selling independent comic of all time. McFarlane later founded McFarlane Toys, securing creative control over his character merchandise and expanding his empire into a major entertainment and collectibles force.
