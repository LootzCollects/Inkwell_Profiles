MARK BROOKS

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: April 12, 1973 (U.S.)
  • Field: Penciller, Cover Artist, Designer
  • Debut: 2001 (10th Muse, Image Comics)
  • Notable Works:
    • Eisner-nominated for Star Wars: Han Solo
    • Cover Artist for Immortal X-Men, Avengers
    • Interior Art on Ultimate Spider-Man Annual, Fearless Defenders

Biography & Influence

Mark Brooks is an Inkpot Award-winning American comic book illustrator and designer, primarily celebrated for his dynamic and highly detailed cover artwork for both Marvel and DC Comics. His early career began in the creator-owned space at Image Comics before a significant shift to Marvel in 2004, where his versatile style quickly made him a fan-favorite.

Brooks' work is recognized for its keen eye for character design and expressive portraiture, which made him a natural fit for titles featuring large ensembles. He has been a prominent force on the X-Men line for years, including critical acclaim for his interiors on the Star Wars: Han Solo miniseries (earning him two Eisner nominations) and his iconic cover runs on books like Avengers: No Surrender and the recent Immortal X-Men.

Beyond traditional comics, Brooks is a sought-after designer who has created signature collectible statue lines for companies like Sideshow Collectibles, further cementing his visual influence across the Marvel universe. His enthusiasm for the medium is evident in his desire to draw the core emotions and relatability of the characters, inspired by classic illustrators and artists like Marc Silvestri.
