AMANDA CONNER

Comic Book Professional

Key Facts

  • Born: March 15, 1967
  • Field: Penciler, Inker, Writer, Cover Artist
  • Debut: 1980s
  • Notable Works:
    • Harley Quinn
    • Power Girl
    • Vampirella

Biography & Influence

Amanda Conner is one of the most recognizable and acclaimed artists in the modern comic book industry, celebrated for her expressive characters, dynamic layouts, and pitch-perfect comedic timing. Initially starting her career in commercial illustration, she transitioned to comics in the late 1980s, finding early success with character work that showcased her versatile ability to handle both drama and lighthearted humor. Her art is instantly identifiable by its energetic line work and highly appealing character designs.

Conner's definitive runs on DC Comics characters have cemented her legacy. Alongside her creative partner (and husband) Jimmy Palmiotti, she revitalized **Power Girl** into a fan-favorite hit and, most notably, reinvented **Harley Quinn** for the New 52 era. Their work on *Harley Quinn* elevated the character to A-list status, emphasizing her anarchic humor and providing a fresh, self-aware take on the former sidekick. Her art style is a masterclass in emotional clarity and cartoonish exaggeration, making every panel feel alive.
