Biography & Influence

Kendrick Lim, known professionally as "Kunkka," is a highly influential illustrator and concept artist who seamlessly bridges the gap between video game art and comic book covers. He began his creative career as a game designer in Tokyo before co-founding **Imaginary Friends Studios (IFS)** in 2005 alongside artists Stanley Lau and **Kai Lim**. IFS quickly established itself as a premier art studio in Asia, producing art for major clients across the gaming, film, and comic industries, including Sony Online Entertainment, Capcom, Square-Enix, and DC Comics.

Kunkka gained immense popularity and acclaim within the gaming community for his dynamic loading screen artwork and official contributions to the Defense of the Ancients (DotA) game series, where his work earned him the high honor of having an official in-game hero, **Admiral Kunkka**, named after him.

In the comic industry, Kunkka is renowned for his detailed and painterly cover illustrations, contributing highly sought-after variant covers to major publishers. He has worked extensively with Marvel, drawing characters like **Carnage**, **Venom**, **Wolverine**, and **Ghost Rider**, and with DC, featuring on titles such as Harley Quinn, **Batman**, and **Poison Ivy**. His style is characterized by high energy, detailed rendering, and a fusion of Western comic sensibilities with Asian illustration aesthetics.
